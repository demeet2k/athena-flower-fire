# Capsule 330 — 15 Canonical Masks

**Source**: 2026-03-18_4d_calculus.md
**Family**: 4d_calculus
**Lens**: S (Square/Structure)

The canonical body is the four-face crystal H = {Square, Flower, Cloud, Fractal} with meanings: Square = structure/embodiment/exact address, Flower = phase/orbit/bloom/turn, Cloud = corridor/ambiguity/liminal admissibility, Fractal = recursion/regeneration/replay. The nonempty face family M = P(H) minus empty set gives 15 canonical masks.

These 15 masks are not cosmetic. They are the first stable nonempty subset-lattice of the crystal and the first exact codifiable field of partial/full embodiment. The canonical enumeration: 01={Sq}, 02={Fl}, 03={Cl}, 04={Fr}, 05={Sq,Fl}, 06={Sq,Cl}, 07={Sq,Fr}, 08={Fl,Cl}, 09={Fl,Fr}, 10={Cl,Fr}, 11={Sq,Fl,Cl}, 12={Sq,Fl,Fr}, 13={Sq,Cl,Fr}, 14={Fl,Cl,Fr}, 15={Sq,Fl,Cl,Fr}.

Each mask represents a different mode of partial or full crystal embodiment. A state with mask {Sq} is purely structural; a state with mask {Fl,Cl} is dynamically admissible but not yet structurally committed or recursively sealed. The full mask {Sq,Fl,Cl,Fr} = 15 is the only complete embodiment.

## Key Objects
- Four faces: Square, Flower, Cloud, Fractal
- 15 canonical masks (nonempty subsets of 4 faces)
- Mask as codification of partial/full embodiment

## Key Laws
- 15 masks are the first stable nonempty subset-lattice of the crystal
- Each mask encodes a specific mode of partial embodiment
- Only mask 15 = {Sq,Fl,Cl,Fr} is complete embodiment
