# InvZ — Fractal Lens / Laws

- `InvZ.R2.a`: `FixedPointExistenceLaw` — By Banach's fixed-point theorem, the contraction mapping F has a unique fixed point (the seed). Existence is guaranteed because the organism's self-replication operator is contractive with ratio 1/φ < 1. The seed exists and is unique.
- `InvZ.R2.b`: `ContractionConvergenceLaw` — Iterating the contraction from any point in the organism's state space converges to the seed at rate 1/φ^n. Convergence is geometric. After n iterations, distance to seed ≤ (1/φ)^n × diameter(organism).
- `InvZ.R2.c`: `MultiplicativeFixedPointLaw` — The Euler product seed is the unique value where multiplicative recursion stabilizes. Adding more prime factors changes the value by less than (1/p^s) — convergence is absolute when Re(s) > 1.
- `InvZ.R2.d`: `RGUniversalityLaw` — At the RG fixed point, the organism's behavior is universal — independent of microscopic details. The seed carries only the universality class, not the specific realization. This is maximum compression: only relevant operators survive.
